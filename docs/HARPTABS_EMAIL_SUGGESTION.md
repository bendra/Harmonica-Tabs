**What HarpPilot does**

HarpPilot is a practice tool for diatonic harmonica players who play in different harp positions. Its main features are:

- A **scale and position reference** — you pick your harp key and the key you want to play in, and the app highlights which holes to use, supporting scales from Major through to Blues Minor and showing diatonic arpeggios and chord voicing
- A **tab library and transposer** — you save tabs as plain text, and the app can transpose them to any key or position and shift them by octave, following along with pitch detection from the phone's microphone as you play

It's a mobile app, built as a passion project. I have no plans to compete with HarpTabs.com or to build a tab library of my own. My intention is explicitly to complement what your site already does, not to replicate or replace it.

**How users currently get tabs from HarpTabs.com into HarpPilot**

Right now the process is manual: a user finds a tab on HarpTabs.com, selects and copies the tab body, then pastes it into HarpPilot's tab editor and saves it to their personal library on their device. The app has a "Clean Input" function that strips any non-tab text that comes along in the paste. The tab is stored locally — nothing is redistributed or published.

I realize that any more assisted import flow is a different question from manual copy and paste. I do not want to assume permission for HarpPilot to extract, copy, or import HarpTabs content in a more automated way. Before adding anything HarpTabs-specific, I wanted to ask you directly and be clear about what I have in mind.

**A question, if you're open to it**

I'd like to ask whether you'd be comfortable with me developing some sort of user-initiated import flow, e.g:

**Search HarpTabs from inside HarpPilot.** A user types a song title in HarpPilot, HarpPilot searches HarpTabs.com, and the user chooses a tab to import into their private local library.

I recognize it may raise concerns, which I am sensitive to.
I'm happy to build in whatever guardrails would make you comfortable. For example:

- imports would be user-initiated only
- imported tabs would be stored only in that user's private local library
- HarpPilot would not publish, redistribute, bundle, or mirror a HarpTabs catalog
- HarpPilot could store the source HarpTabs URL with imported tabs and show attribution plus a link back to the original HarpTabs page
- HarpPilot would not cache HarpTabs page content on a server unless you explicitly approved that

I accept that such an assisted flow would involve HarpPilot extracting content from your site, which may go beyond what your current terms permit. I completely understand if you're not comfortable with this. If you are open in principle but not to the flow outlined above, or if you would want conditions such as attribution, rate limiting, source links, a formal agreement, or something else, I'd be glad to discuss that. I have no expectation that you'll say yes, and the app works well without this feature.

If it would help, I'm happy to share a build of the app for you to try, or to answer any questions about how it works.

Thanks for running HarpTabs.com — it's a genuinely valuable resource for the harmonica community.
