**Importing tabs from HarpTabs.com**

HarpTabs.com is a large community library of harmonica tabs. Most tabs there are key-agnostic: the numbers refer to hole positions on any diatonic harp, and you choose the harp key to suit your situation.

To copy a tab into HarpPilot:

- Find your song on HarpTabs.com and select the tab you want. Tabs marked § are diatonic.
- Select and copy just the tab body — the lines of numbers and lyrics. You can skip the song header (title, artist, key, difficulty) and any footnotes.
- In HarpPilot, go to Tabs → Library and tap **New Tab**. Give it a title, paste the copied text into the editor, then tap **Clean Input** to strip any non-tab content that came along for the ride.
- Tap **Save**.

The tab is now in your library and can be opened in the Transposer. Since the source tab has no key attached, HarpPilot will treat it as a neutral reference — the numbers mean the same thing on any harp. To play it:

- Pick up whichever harp fits the song's key (or the key the band is in).
- In the Transposer top row, set **Harmonica Key** to that harp and **Target Position/Key** to the position you want to play in. 1st position means the tab plays in the harp's own key; 2nd position transposes it up a fifth (classic blues cross-harp). The displayed tab updates live.
- Use **Down / Up** to shift the tab an octave if the melody sits too high or low on your harp.

**Tip:** If the key-agnostic tab was written for a C harp (common on HarpTabs), the numbers will feel most natural without any transposition. If you're playing a different harp in 1st position, just set Harmonica Key to match and leave Target Position/Key at 1st — the tab will read correctly as-is.

---

A couple of notes on what I included and why: I kept the key-agnostic framing front and centre because that's the actual mental model — the tab is just a shape, and the harp choice gives it a key. I also flagged the § diatonic marker because that's the relevant filter for this app, and it's the kind of detail the existing help already gives (it mentions specific UI labels and settings rather than being vague). The tip at the end addresses the "C harp assumption" that's very common on harptabs.com without making it a rule.
